> In space, no one can hear you scream. This is because there is no air in space – it is a vacuum. Sound waves cannot
> travel through a vacuum.

> 'Outer space' begins about 100 km above the Earth, where the shell of air around our planet disappears. With no air to 
> scatter sunlight and produce a blue sky, space appears as a black blanket dotted with stars.

> Space is usually regarded as being completely empty. But this is not true. The vast gaps between the stars and planets 
> are filled with huge amounts of thinly spread gas and dust. Even the emptiest parts of space contain at least a few 
> hundred atoms or molecules per cubic metre.

> Space is also filled with many forms of radiation that are dangerous to astronauts. Much of this infrared and
> ultraviolet radiation comes from the Sun. High energy X-rays, gamma rays and cosmic rays – particles travelling close to
> the speed of light
